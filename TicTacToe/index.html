How to Play
1
Take turns placing marks
X moves first, then O. Tap any empty square to place your mark.
2
Get three in a row to win
Line up three of your marks horizontally, vertically, or diagonally. The golden line celebrates your victory.
3
Block your opponent
If they get two in a row, place your next mark in the third spot to stop them — defense wins matches.
4
Fill the board → draw
If all nine squares fill with no winner, the round ends in a stalemate. The starting player alternates each round.
5
Keyboard shortcuts
Use the numpad 1–9 to play (numpad layout matches the board). Press R for a new round, Esc for menu.
Starter Tips
⊕
Take the center first
If you go first, claim the middle square. It's part of 4 winning lines — more than any other square. Corners give you 3, edges only 2.
◣
Corners beat edges
When the center is taken, grab a corner. Edges are the weakest squares — avoid them unless you're forced or blocking a threat.
⚠
Always block 2-in-a-row
If the opponent has two marks lined up with the third square open, you must block — otherwise they win next turn.
⚡
Build a fork
A fork is a move that creates two threats at once. Your opponent can only block one — you win with the other. The classic fork: opposite corners.

⊖
If they take center, take a corner
Going second against a center opening? Pick a corner. It denies them the easy double-threat and keeps you in the game.
🛡
Watch for opponent's forks
Don't just defend the obvious threat — check if your "block" lets them set up a fork. Sometimes you must play offense to dodge a trap.
∞
Perfect play = draw
Tic Tac Toe is "solved": with optimal play from both sides, the game always ends in a draw. Impossible mode plays this perfect strategy. Best you can do is tie — every win against it is your opponent making a mistake.
